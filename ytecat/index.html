The World

One hundred years ago, a portal to the Demon Realm tore open in the world of Ytecat. The seven races — already weakened from centuries of war against each other — were overwhelmed. Today, eighty-three percent of their world is occupied. What remains is held not through strength, but through sheer refusal to stop.

83% World Occupied
100 Years Since the Portal
7 Races of Ytecat

Haruki and Hayate are the only survivors of their village. They didn't escape — they fought their way out, and the weight of everyone they couldn't save has shaped them in opposite ways. Haruki became calm, methodical, the steady hand his brother needed. Hayate became something harder — aggressive, instinct-driven, carrying grief as rage.

In a world where the seven races distrust each other almost as much as they fear the demons, this group of misfits begins to do something quietly dangerous — trust each other.

Together they arrive at Akebono with borrowed swords and empty pockets, looking for work. What they find instead is a party — an elf archer with warmth on the surface and darkness underneath, a Cecaelian warrior searching for a missing brother, and a stoic Beastman who protects the ones no one else will.

But the world is not content to let them grow at their own pace. A demon spy embedded in the closest human town has been watching them since the beginning — using them, manipulating them, and when they proved too useful to simply eliminate, sending them behind enemy lines on a job designed to serve his agenda whether they survive it or not.

The Seven Races

Humans
Adaptable and numerous, humans are scattered across what remains of the free world. Their greatest strength is also their oldest flaw — they are capable of both extraordinary unity and devastating division.
Elves
Long-lived and perceptive, elves carry centuries of memory — including every war they've fought and every alliance that has failed them. Warmth, when they offer it, is earned slowly and guarded carefully.
Cecaelians
Cecaelians are oceanic beings — part humanoid, part cephalopod — powerful in both body and will. They are fiercely loyal to family above all else, and the loss of one of their own leaves a wound that does not heal.
Beastmen
Varied in form and fierce in spirit, Beastmen are defined less by their appearance than their code — protect those who cannot protect themselves. Their stoicism is often mistaken for coldness. It is not.
Dwarves
Builders and survivors, dwarves have held their underground strongholds longer than any other race. Their endurance is legendary. Their patience with the failures of others is considerably less so.
Revenants
Something brought them back — and whatever it was, it did not return them whole. Revenants exist between states, neither fully living nor fully gone. In a world already on the edge of ruin, they are a reminder that some costs cannot be undone.
Orcs
Orcs have survived a hundred years of occupation through invisibility — moving quietly, trading carefully, and knowing when to vanish. Do not mistake their discretion for weakness.
Demons
They came through a portal no one asked for and have not stopped since. The demon army is disciplined, patient and vast — and behind its generals lies something worse: intelligence that has been watching the free races for a very long time.

The Party

Haruki
Human  •  Swordsman
The older brother. He became calm and methodical because someone had to — because Hayate couldn't, and someone had to be the steady hand. He carries the same grief as his brother. He just wears it differently.
"The weight of the ones we couldn't save."
Hayate
Human  •  Swordsman
The younger brother. Aggressive, instinct-driven, and running from something he can't outrun. He carries grief as rage because rage at least feels like it's going somewhere. By the end of Season 1, he learns what it costs to be the steady one.
"Carrying grief as rage."
The Elf
Elf  •  Archer
Warmth on the surface and something darker underneath. She is easy to like and careful to be known. The party will have to earn what she hasn't shown them yet.
"Warmth on the surface, darkness underneath."
The Cecaelian
Cecaelian  •  Guardian
Armed with a magic trident that returns to her hand when thrown and a shield that has turned more blows than she can count. Every fight she takes, every risk she accepts — it is all in service of one thing. Finding her missing brother. The party are means to an end, until they aren't.
"Searching for what was taken."
The Beastman
Beastman  •  Assassin
Stoic and deliberate, he has made a quiet decision about what he is for. He protects the ones no one else will. The party didn't ask for a guardian. They got one anyway.
"Protects the ones no one else will."
